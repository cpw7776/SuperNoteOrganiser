# Original Thoughts

## On boredom as a signal

Boredom isn't the absence of stimulation — it's the mind telling you the
current task no longer earns your attention. Treat it as a routing signal:
when bored, don't reach for the phone, ask "what does this boredom mean my
attention should be on instead?"

## On reading widely vs deeply

Reading one good book three times beats reading three mediocre books once.
The marginal returns of a familiar text rise the deeper you go, because you
stop spending cycles on parsing and start spending them on synthesis. Pick
fewer books, return more often.
